What changes when you measure instead of estimate?

Free — power / pace-only profile

VLamax is back-derived from sprint power via an empirical regression. VO₂max comes from your 6-minute max effort, anchored to Léger's relation. Useful as a directional read on your training zones — but real VLamax varies a lot at any given sprint output, and those errors cascade through to MLSS, Fatmax, and substrate-burning estimates.

Paid — lactate-anchored profile

VLamax is measured from blood lactate at peak sprint effort, and the full lactate curve from your step test pins down MLSS, LT1, and Fatmax directly. Same Mader / Heck bioenergetic model — the one INSCYD, Aerotune, and sports-science labs use — anchored to your actual physiology instead of population averages.
